About This Archive

Our Mission

This archive is dedicated to preserving the complete history of MTV's Headbangers Ball, the iconic heavy metal television program that aired from 1987 to 2012. Our goal is to document every episode, host appearance, and special moment from this legendary show that introduced generations of fans to metal music.

Through meticulous research and cross-referencing of multiple sources, we've created the most comprehensive database of Headbangers Ball episodes available anywhere on the internet.

About Headbangers Ball

Headbangers Ball premiered on MTV on April 18, 1987, and quickly became the premier destination for heavy metal music videos and interviews. The show gave mainstream exposure to bands that traditionally received little airplay, helping to launch and sustain careers in the metal community.

Show Eras

1987-1995
Classic MTV Era

The original run featured legendary hosts including Kevin Seal, Adam Curry, and the iconic Riki Rachtman. This era captured the golden age of metal, from thrash to hair metal to the rise of grunge.

2003-2007
MTV2 Revival

The show returned on MTV2 with hosts like Jamey Jasta, featuring modern metal, metalcore, and nu-metal acts that defined the early 2000s scene.

2011-2012
Final Revival

A brief final revival brought the show back one last time before concluding its remarkable 25-year legacy.

Research & Methodology

Episode data has been compiled from multiple verified sources:

  • TheTVDB.com: Comprehensive episode listings and air dates
  • Archive.org: Preserved episodes and historical records
  • Metal Video Collector Archives: Fan-preserved episode guides
  • Collector Archives: Private collections and episode documentation
  • Contemporary Publications: MTV guides, magazines, and press materials

Each episode entry is cross-referenced across multiple sources to ensure accuracy. Where exact details are unavailable, we've used historical broadcast patterns and contemporary documentation to estimate missing information.

Technology

This archive is built using modern web technologies to ensure fast, accessible, and sustainable preservation of Headbangers Ball history:
